Creamy Garlic Parmesan Pasta

A rich, silky pasta sauce made with garlic, parmesan, and cream — ready in 25 minutes and better than takeout.

🍝
Prep Time10 min
Cook Time15 min
Servings4
DifficultyEasy

Ingredients

  • 12 oz spaghetti or fettuccine
  • 3 tbsp butter
  • 5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 1/2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup grated parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the pasta until al dente, according to package directions. Reserve 1 cup of pasta water, then drain.
  2. While the pasta cooks, melt the butter in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the garlic and cook for 1 minute, until fragrant — don't let it brown.
  3. Pour in the heavy cream and bring to a gentle simmer. Cook for 3–4 minutes, stirring occasionally, until slightly thickened.
  4. Reduce heat to low and stir in the parmesan cheese a handful at a time, until melted and smooth. Season with red pepper flakes, salt, and pepper.
  5. Add the drained pasta to the skillet and toss to coat, adding splashes of the reserved pasta water as needed to loosen the sauce.
  6. Top with extra parmesan and fresh parsley. Serve immediately.

Notes

For extra protein, toss in grilled chicken or sautéed shrimp before serving. Leftovers keep well for up to 3 days — reheat gently with a splash of milk or cream.
