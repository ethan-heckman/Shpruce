Loaded Veggie Stir-Fry

A quick, colorful stir-fry with a savory garlic-ginger sauce — endlessly customizable with whatever's in the fridge.

🥦
Prep Time15 min
Cook Time10 min
Servings4
DifficultyEasy

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 broccoli head, cut into florets
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 carrot, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup snap peas
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, minced
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tbsp honey or brown sugar
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp cornstarch mixed with 2 tbsp water
  • Sesame seeds and green onion, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Whisk together the soy sauce, rice vinegar, honey, and sesame oil in a small bowl. Set aside.
  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a large wok or skillet over high heat until shimmering.
  3. Add the broccoli and carrot first, stir-frying for 2–3 minutes.
  4. Add the bell pepper and snap peas, and stir-fry for another 2–3 minutes, until the vegetables are crisp-tender.
  5. Push the vegetables to the side, add the garlic and ginger to the center, and cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  6. Pour in the sauce and toss everything together. Add the cornstarch slurry and cook for 1 minute, until the sauce thickens and coats the vegetables.
  7. Garnish with sesame seeds and green onion. Serve over rice or noodles.

Notes

This recipe is very forgiving — swap in any vegetables you have, like mushrooms, zucchini, or baby corn. For added protein, toss in cubed tofu or sliced chicken before adding the vegetables.
