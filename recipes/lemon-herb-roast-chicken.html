Lemon Herb Roast Chicken

A juicy, golden roast chicken with lemon, garlic, and fresh herbs — simple enough for a weeknight, special enough for Sunday.

🍗
Prep Time20 min
Cook Time1 hr 10 min
Servings6
DifficultyMedium

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken (4–5 lbs)
  • 1 lemon, halved
  • 1 head garlic, halved crosswise
  • 4 tbsp butter, softened
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tsp fresh thyme, chopped (or 1 tsp dried)
  • 2 tsp fresh rosemary, chopped (or 1 tsp dried)
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 2 carrots, roughly chopped
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C). Remove the chicken from the fridge 30 minutes before roasting to come to room temperature.
  2. Pat the chicken completely dry with paper towels — this is the key to crispy skin.
  3. Mix the softened butter with the thyme, rosemary, salt, and pepper. Rub the herb butter under and over the skin of the chicken.
  4. Stuff the cavity with the halved lemon and garlic head. Tie the legs together with kitchen twine, if desired.
  5. Scatter the onion and carrots in a roasting pan, drizzle with olive oil, and place the chicken on top, breast-side up.
  6. Roast for 60–75 minutes, until the internal temperature at the thickest part of the thigh reaches 165°F (74°C) and the juices run clear.
  7. Let the chicken rest for 10–15 minutes before carving.

Notes

Basting isn't necessary if the skin is well-buttered, but you can spoon pan juices over the chicken halfway through for extra flavor. Save the carcass for homemade stock.
